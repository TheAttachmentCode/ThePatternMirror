What this tool is — and what it isn't

This tool isn't here to label you or diagnose your relationship.

Words like anxious or avoidant are simply shorthand for patterns people develop when they are trying to protect themselves emotionally.

What we are really exploring is something much simpler:

The pattern that hurts.
The moment where something happens between you and another person and suddenly your body reacts.
Maybe you feel anxious. Maybe you shut down. Maybe you become defensive or overwhelmed.

None of those responses mean something is wrong with you.

They simply mean something inside you learned how to stay safe.

Most people try to avoid these moments because they feel uncomfortable.

But avoiding the pattern doesn't stop it from happening.

It simply means it will keep repeating.

Often in different relationships. Often in the same relationship. Sometimes many times a week. Or even in one day.

This reflection invites you to pause for a few minutes and look at the pattern directly.

Because when you understand the pattern clearly, something changes.

What once felt overwhelming begins to make sense.

And what makes sense becomes easier to transform.

Before you begin

This tool only works if you answer honestly.

Not according to who you believe you should be, or who you hope to become — but according to how you actually tend to respond when emotions are real and the moment is unfolding.

The pattern can only show itself when you tell the truth about what really happens. There is no right answer here. There is only what is real for you.

No one else will see this. It exists only for you.

How do you tend to respond in this relationship?

Choose the options that feel most honest — not ideal, but real.

Now answer the same reflections for the other person.

Based on how they tend to respond — from what you have observed, felt, or experienced in this relationship.

This is your perception of their pattern — not a diagnosis. You can only ever see another person's behaviour through your own nervous system. Be honest, but hold it lightly.
